Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering




The Mitchell Institute Neutrino Experiment at Reactor (MINER) is a short-baseline neutrino experiment at the Nuclear Science Center (NSC) at Texas A\(\&\)M University. MINER proposes to probe (neutrino physics) coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CNS) using ultra-low energy threshold cryogenic germanium detectors. Though CNS is a strong theoretical prediction of the Standard Model (SM), it has been observed only very recently, and never in electron neutrinos. Most importantly, low threshold detectors will drastically increase neutrino detection rates as CNS has a cross-section much larger than the traditional inverse beta-decay processes of neutrinos. Additionally, CNS is also inescapable background in direct dark matter searches.

MINER Sensitivity to CNS



The germanium and silicon detectors will be placed $2-10$ m away from the reactor in the NSC. We would be using about \(5-20\) kg of cryogenic germanium detector$-$adapted from the SuperCDMS collaboration$-$with thresholds <100 eV. Being in the presence of the reactor core exposes are detector to a very large background flux of neutron and gamma particles. As we want to isolate CNS events, we would shield our detectors appropriately from these fluxes to increase the ratio between signal and background rate, \((\frac{S}{B})\). Analysis of MINER's backgrounds is done in [2]. After shielding, MINER expects an \((\frac{S}{B})\) on the order of 1.

Analysis of MINER's sensitivity illustrates that within reasonable experimental constraints, MINER can discover CNS in electron neutrinos and determine its cross-section within a few months worth of runtime.

Figure 2: Time evolution of \(\chi^2\) for specific experimental parameters.

Fig. 2 shows how the chi-square improves with time and how the improvement changes when one experimental parameter varies. With a detector threshold around $50$ eV, $(\frac{S}{B})\sim 0.5-1$ and systematic error limited to about 20\(\%\), MINER can discover CNS with 3-\(\sigma\) in a few weeks to two months. If we consider energy information of the neutrinos and perform a shape-based analysis, MINER can discover CNS much sooner and get a 5-\(\sigma\) discovery in a month. Similarly, Fig. 3 shows the improvement of the cross-section measurement precision with time. We see that the precision gets systematically limited in just a few months.

Figure 3: Improvement of CNS cross-section precision \((\frac{d\sigma}{\sigma})\) with time.

Deviations of CNS from the standard model would be windows to possible new physics beyond the standard model. The ultra-low threshold detectors in this reactor setup can also be used to probe such processes. MINER also shows sensitivity to hypothetical \(4^{th}\) \(\textit{sterile neutrino}\) oscillations [3] and \(\textit{neutrino decay}\) processes [4].
